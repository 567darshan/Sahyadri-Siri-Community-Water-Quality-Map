# Sahyadri Siri – Community Water Quality Map

## Project Overview
Sahyadri Siri is an Android application developed for monitoring and reporting water quality conditions of streams, sacred springs, and natural water bodies in the Western Ghats (Sahyadri region).

The application allows users to submit real-time reports about water clarity, pollution level, smell, and flow conditions. The collected community data is used to create a Water Health Monitoring System that helps identify polluted water sources early.

This project focuses on environmental protection, water conservation, and public health awareness using Android and cloud technologies.

---

# Problem Statement
In many villages and forest regions of the Western Ghats, local communities depend on streams and springs for drinking water. There is currently no centralized digital system to monitor water quality.

If water pollution occurs upstream, downstream villagers often realize the issue only after diseases or contamination spread. Therefore, an intelligent community-based monitoring application is required for early pollution detection and awareness.

---

# Objectives of the Project
- Develop an Android application for water quality monitoring.
- Collect water condition reports from local users.

4. System processes water status.
5. Water Health Map updates.
6. Alerts are generated if pollution is detected.
